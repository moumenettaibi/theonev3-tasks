The Philosophy of Time

The philosophy of time is a branch of philosophy that deals with the ontological and epistemological nature of time. Key questions include: What is time? Is it an illusion? Does time have a direction? Major theories include presentism (only the present exists), eternalism (past, present, and future are equally real), and the A-series and B-series theories proposed by J.M.E. McTaggart. These concepts are often explored in conjunction with physics, metaphysics, and logic.
